Disabled Students Program

The Disabled Students’ program also known as DSP is designed to guarantee that students with disabilities have equitable access to UC Berkeley's academic programs. In addition, DSP provides support for students inside the classroom, their office provides necessary Auxiliary Services to let students participate equally in the learning environment. Services may include note-taking technologies, different course text formats, real-time course captioning, and unique test arrangements.

Represents, helps, and gives advice to any student/group involved with a dispute with the University. Advice given from the SAO is free and confidential to all UC Students. They also have drop-in Zoom hours from 10am to 5pm with a virtual caseworker

Ensures all building at UCB are in compliance with the Americans With Disabilities Act. Helps the University to go out of their way to make the campus more accessible. Fosters a campus community for disabled students. Students can voice their concerns to the DAC through their website!
