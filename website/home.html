Welcome to Cal! This website was created by the Office of Senator Issabella Romo specifically for new freshmen and transfer students, to better help transition into your life at Cal. We hope that you can take advantage of all the resources listed, to ensure a smooth transition both in the classroom and in the community.

Academic Resources

These resources help showcase the abundance of academic help resources that students are able to utilize across the UC Berkeley campus. These resources are available largely through the Student Learning Center (SLC), which hosts the largest number of tutors and resources for students that need help in their classes. Additionally, all Residence Halls have programs that are geared towards helping students academically, through tutoring, advising, and holding spaces for residents to study.

Financial Aid Resources

These resources are for students that receive financial assistance from UC Berkeley. This page can help navigate you through the plethora of options available to students, such as applying for work-study and preparation for filing taxes. In addition to these, the legal services can also offer help to students consultants on taking legal actions, as well as hosting free legal advice sessions for all students.

Technology Resources

The Student Tech Services also known as STS, is designed to ensure that all students at Berkeley have access to technological resources. This is a wide range consisting of device lending, tech support, remote access, and computer access to all campus libraries.

Tutoring Resources

The page here has resources for the students struggling with courses and are in need of academic support. Learn about the Student Learning Center, which provides free tutoring for specific courses. Berkeley Connect is another good resource available which involves more personal mentoring and peer advising sessions. This tutoring option is perfect to meet new people and master the courses you are struggling in at the same time.

EOP Resources

Everything here has services available to the undergraduates who are first-generation, low-income, or underrepresented students. Learn about what the EOP academic counselors can provide you with. Whether it be academic planning, financial assistance, or graduate school/career exploration, the counselors are here to help. The page will also provide information regarding who is eligible to be an EOP student.

DSP Resources

The Disabled Studentsâ€™ program also known as DSP is designed to guarantee that students with disabilities have equitable access to UC Berkeley's academic programs. In addition, DSP provides support for students inside the classroom, their office provides necessary Auxiliary Services to let students participate equally in the learning environment. Services may include note-taking technologies, different course text formats, real-time course captioning, and unique test arrangements.

Mental Health Resources

All the resources included here are related to mental health. Learn some self-care tips as we all face challenges during a pandemic. Additionally, if you are in need of support during your time here at Cal, there are many counseling, social nutrition, and psychological services available. Whether or not it be an emergency, we offer in-person and group counseling, so please do not hesitate to use these resources because everyone deserves healing.

Sexual Health Resources

This page holds resources that help Cal students maintain their sexual health throughout their time in Berkeley. There is information on how to get tested for different STIs, HIV, and pregnancy. In addition to this, UC Berkeley also provides health coaching to different students, which is individualized to each student seeking these resources. Additionally, the UHS Pharmacy can also help supply students with contraceptives and other sex-related supplies for a low cost.

Communities at Cal Resources

On this page, you can find information on all of the different communities that Cal has to offer. There are specialized resources for members of the LGBTQ+ community, women, Black students, AAPI students, and Latine students. These resources are able to help students find a sense of belonging at Cal, and meet like-minded individuals. Additionally, you can find information on the Multicultural Community Center here.

Housing Resources

The following page has resources and relevant information pertaining to housing on and off campus. Learn how to navigate the various housing options offered by the university, including but not limited to dorms, co-ops, and apartment living. Additionally, one can learn about the rights they have as tenants by visiting links to the Rent Stabilization Board as well as the Berkeley Tenants Union.
