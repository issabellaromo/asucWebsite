Communities at Cal

There are many communities on campus that are available to make students feel welcome. The Queer and Trans Resources are available to help anyone in need of support, whether it be joining LGBTQ+ organizations or counseling. There is a Queer Alliance Resrouces Center available to provide all the resources and oganizations to the LGBTQ+ in order to bring them together. Women’s resources are plenty as well. Any woman is welcome to participate in the programs and services available in order to make women feel not so marginalized and make them feel like they belong. For the men, there are resources that include counseling, organizations regarding men and feminism, and other groups that have men be allies to LGBT people. For the black students, there are plenty of organizations available. Many are academic focused or are more intended to meet people within the same community. The Asian American and Pacific Islander students have student organizations that include empowerment for the underserved Asian/Pacific Islanders, student development as well as advocating civil rights, and guidance for the Asian/Pacific Islanders considering law school. The resources for Latine are very diverse. Some organizations could be like Ballet Folklorico Reflejos De Mexico, a Mexican folkloric dance group or organizations that are about advocacy and address social issues along with cultural issues Latine students at Berkeley face. Additionally, the Multicultural Community Center is a student-led space to help build cross/intercultural communities. Overall, there are plenty of organizations that suit anyone’s interests, whether it be academic or community based.
