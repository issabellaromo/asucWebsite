Sexual Violence and Sexual Harrassment

UC Berkeley provides many resources pertaining to SVSH. The University Health Services (UHS)/Tang Center in the section of Social Services provides free individual counseling and consultation for students. Berkeley also provides service lines that can help assist individuals who need consultation or aid in after-hours. The OMBUDS Office for Students and Postdoctoral Appointees provides a confidential, neutral resource for navigating issues with the university, that give informal and formal options, with the goal of empowering students to make choices that best fit their needs. They are strictly confidential and will not participate or insinuate any legal proceedings. The Student Advocates Office (SAO) provides an opportunity for undergraduate and graduate students to work with a variety of caseworkers who can help them understand a variety of resources they have access to relating to any of their issues (academic, financial...etc). The SAO is a fully confidential program that is an off-campus resource. The Family Violence Law Center (FVLC) provides legal assistance to individuals who have dealt/deal with domestic violence and sexual assault. A local program that supports Cal students is Bay Area Women against rape, who provide local counseling, hospital, police, and courtroom accompaniment for sexual violence survivors of all genders. All services are confidential and free. It's important to take note that whatever resources the individual feels comfortable with choosing to utilize is ultimately up to their discretion. The Office for Precention of Harrasement & Discrimination ensures that the University provides an environment that is free from any form of discrimination, harrasement, and sexual violence. The PATH to care caters aid to individuals who have experienced any harm relating to sexual assault, dating and relationship violence, sexual harassment, stalking, and invasion of privacy PATH to Care aims to create an affirming and empowering environment with confidential support for those who have experienced all forms of sexual harassment and emotional abuse. It is a confidential program whose advocates bring a non-judgmental, caring approach to exploring all options, rights, and resources. At any emergency, Berkeley students can undoubtedly call the UC Berkeley Police Department.

PATH to Care Center
Affirming, empowering, and confidential support for those who have experienced sexual harassment, emotional abuse, dating and intimate partner violence, sexual assault, stalking, and sexual exploitation. Confidential advocates in the PATH to Care Center bring a non-judgmental, caring approach to exploring all options, rights, and resources.

PATH to Care serves anyone in the UC Berkeley community who has experienced harm related to sexual assault, dating and relationship violence, sexual harassment, stalking, and invasion of privacy (including current and former undergraduate and graduate/professional students, faculty, staff, visiting scholars, and those who were harmed by someone affiliated with UC Berkeley), even if the incident occurred prior to coming to UC Berkeley or off campus.
