Housing

If you are a UC Berkeley student looking into living off campus and are in search of housing options, there are several resources that the school provides. The Berkeley Rent Stabilization Board page aids Cal students to understand their rights as tenants and their responsibilities under certain laws in the City of Berkeley and Alameda County. It also provides students with the knowledge of understanding the laws of being a landlord or tenant. There are a few resources that can help students navigate their options of housing. The Berkeley Graduate Division provides tips for finding housing applying to all topics relating to long and short term housing, finding roommates, neighborhoods...etc. The Berkeley Student Cooperative provides information on their Co-Op housing, where priority is given to EOP, DSP, Undocumented and Transfer students. Cal Rentals additionally provides information on rentals, summer sublets, roommates and buying and selling items. Additionally, Cal Rentals provides information on off-campus housing, student support services and housing assistance for students with disabilities.
